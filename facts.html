Humans are the primary cause of global warming.


How much do you know about global warming?

Greenhouse gas emissions are the key factor to global warming. While greenhouse gases are fine in moderation, we are ultimately creating an excess of man-made greenhouse gases that absorb heat and radiation from the sun. Our ozone layer is trapping this heat and radiation in our atmosphere at an exponential rate.

Since the industrial revolution, excessive greenhouse gases have been emitted by human creations, causing the increase in temperature in our atmosphere.

THE CAUSES

(Hover for more info)

Livestock Farming

Livestock release methane when they digest food, especially cows and cattle.

Transportation

Burning fossil fuels produce a large amount of carbon oxide.

Manufacturing and Industrial pollution

GHG emissions include carbon dioxide, nitrous oxide and methane.

Garbage Disposal & Waste Dumping

Landfills produce methane and release it into the air.

Fertilizers

The use of fertlizers in agriculture produces nitrous oxide, which traps 300 times more heat than CO2.

Coolants & Refrigerants

Coolants/refrigerants emit chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs), which destroy the ozone layer in the stratosphere.
